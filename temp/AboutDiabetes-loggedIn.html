About Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic diease that affects how your body transforms food into energy

    • What is Diabetes?

    Diabetes is a serious complex condition which can affect the entire body. Diabetes requires daily self care and if complications develop, diabetes can have a significiant impact on quality of life and can reduce life expectancy. While there is currently no cure for diabetes, you can live an enjoyable life by learning about the condition and effectively managing it.

    Type 1 Diabetes

    Type 1 Diabetes is an autoimmune condition in which the immune system is activated to desotry the cells in the pancreas which produce insulin. Type 1 Diabetes is not linked to modifiable lifestyle factors. There is no cure and it cannot be prevented.

    Consult your doctor if you notice any symptoms in you or your child.

    Type 2 Diabetes

    Type 2 diabetes is a condition in which the body becomes resistant to the normal effects of insulin and gradually loses the capacity to produce enough insulin in the pancreas. The condition has strong genetic and family related (non-modifiable) risk factors and is also often associated with modifiable lifestyle risk factors.

    Is common for people with type 2 diabetes to display no symptoms. In some cases,symptoms may be present. Consult you doctor if you experience any possible symptoms.

    Gestational Diabetes

    Gestational diabetes mellitus (sometimes referred to as GDM) is a form of diabetes that occurs during pregnancy. Most women will no longer have diabetes after the baby is born. However, some women will continue to have high blood glucose levels after deliver. It is diagnosed when higher than normal blood glucose levels first appear during pregnancy. In most cases, gestational diabetes doesn't show noticeable signs or symptoms. Increased thirst and more frequent urination are possible symptoms.
